AMD + IFVG model

Accumulation → Manipulation → IFVG → Distribution

Wait for range build, fake move, imbalance flip, then expansion.

A
Accumulation Price moves sideways and builds orders. Do not force trades inside the chop.
M
Manipulation Price sweeps one side of the range to trap early buyers or sellers.
I
IFVG confirmation An imbalance fails and flips direction. This is your confirmation area, not the first wick.
D
Distribution Price expands away from the range. Manage TP into liquidity and protect profit.

Strategy 01

AMD + IFVG Reversal

First, let price accumulate inside a tight range. Then wait for manipulation, usually a sweep above or below the range. After that, look for an IFVG or imbalance flip that shows the trap failed. The entry is best on the retest of that IFVG area, with stop loss beyond the manipulation wick and target toward the opposite liquidity.

Strategy 02

Sweep + BOS Pullback

This is the safer version of catching reversals. Do not enter on the sweep candle. Wait for price to break structure in the new direction. After the BOS, wait for a pullback into a clean area. This avoids entering too early and helps reduce fakeout trades.

Strategy 03

Liquidity Sweep + Breaker Retest

This is the extra strategy. After price sweeps liquidity and breaks structure, find the last failed candle or small zone that caused the break. That area can become a breaker. If price comes back to retest it and rejects, enter with stop behind the breaker and target the next liquidity pool.
